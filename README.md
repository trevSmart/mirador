# Mirador

**See your contact center at a glance.**

Mirador is a real-time supervision dashboard for contact centers running on
Salesforce. It gives supervisors a single, live view of everything happening on
the floor — who's available, what's queuing, and where work is going — so they
can act on it while it still matters.

## What it does

- **Live agent monitoring** — presence, availability, and current workload for
  every agent, updated in real time. Spot who's overloaded and who's idle at a
  glance.
- **Queue oversight** — see backlog, wait times, and throughput across every
  queue, so bottlenecks surface before they become escalations.
- **Work in flight** — track individual work items and how they're being routed
  and assigned, with the detail a supervisor needs to intervene.
- **Skills view** — understand how skills and expertise map to demand, and why
  work lands where it does.
- **The floor, visualized** — an isometric "space plan" of the contact center
  turns the raw numbers into a picture of the room: seats, agents, and activity
  laid out spatially.
- **A workspace you arrange** — every view is a panel you can dock, split, and
  rearrange into the layout that fits how you work. Your layout is remembered.

Mirador is built to match — and go beyond — the standard Salesforce supervisor
experience (Omni Supervisor / Command Center for Service), with a faster, more
focused interface designed to be watched all day.

## How it works

Mirador reads live data directly from your Salesforce org and presents it in a
purpose-built dashboard. Agents, queues, skills, and work items stay in sync
automatically, refreshing in the background so what's on screen is always
current.

Access is secured through Salesforce's standard sign-in — supervisors log in
with their existing org credentials, and Mirador only ever shows data they're
already permitted to see.

## Getting started

Mirador connects to your Salesforce org. Talk to your Salesforce administrator
to have it enabled for your team, then sign in with your usual Salesforce
credentials — no separate account required.
